This morning after breakfast, a few of us from the dorm headed back to our room. Agu spotted a senior selling his old computer—a Celeron junk CPU, 80G junk hard drive, a thousand bucks. We figured we'd go check out just how junky it really was. After getting lost once, we finally found the guy. Agu's a computer whiz; the second he touched it, he had the thing half-paralyzed. While he was working on it, the senior suddenly jumped up yelling "Earthquake!" The guy in the bathroom poked his head out too—probably mid-squat—and asked if it was an earthquake. No idea how he handled... that. Agu kept fiddling with the computer. Zhang Zhen is from Mianyang; he must've been terrified. He came here specifically to escape the earthquake. I didn't feel anything—maybe I'm just numb. I went to the window and looked down. People below were panicking. We kept looking at the computer and ignored the quake. In the end Agu decided it was truly fucking junk, so we went downstairs. Damn it, fucking everything.

After getting downstairs we headed back to the dorm. There's one thing I have to say: I'm really fucking pissed. Why the hell do we live on the goddamn tenth floor? Climbing those stairs is murder. Our school is so cheap they won't fix the elevator and put the main entrance on the fourth floor just to avoid it. We finally made it up, and Li Yunhong was coming down. Even though he's from Chongqing (I refuse to admit Chongqing isn't part of Sichuan—we've argued about this; he always points to the sign on the Chengdu-Chongqing Expressway that says "Welcome Sichuan People to Chongqing"), he took the college entrance exam in Xinjiang and missed the May 12 earthquake. He said everyone in the building had already run out, so why were we going up? At his urging I grabbed my stuff and followed them back downstairs. I was planning to head back to Chengdu—right then it genuinely felt like fleeing. Felt bleak.

They went out with Li to buy speakers. He bought a computer yesterday but didn't get speakers, just a pair of earbuds. Fuck that.
